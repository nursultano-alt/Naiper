    About Napier's Bones

    Napier's Bones are a manually operated calculating device created by John Napier of Merchiston, first published in 1617.

    The device consists of a board with a rim and a set of numbered rods (the "bones"). Each rod contains a single-digit multiplication table. To multiply, you lay out the rods for each digit of the multiplicand and read off the row that corresponds to your multiplier, adding the diagonal columns to obtain the final product.

    This application automates that process — enter any multiplicand and multiplier to see the bones laid out and the step-by-step addition.
